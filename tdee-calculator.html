EVIDENCE-BASED NUTRITION & METABOLISM

Understanding Metabolic Rates and Evidence-Based Nutrition for Athletic and General Populations

Your metabolism is not a mystery; it is math. The Mifflin-St Jeor equation — validated as the most accurate macro calculator for weight loss foundation — calculates Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), the energy required for cellular function at rest. For men: BMR = 10×weight(kg) + 6.25×height(cm) − 5×age +5; for women, −161. Multiplying BMR by activity factor (1.2 sedentary to 1.9 very active) yields Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE), the true daily calorie needs global clinicians use for weight management, sports dietetics, and post-operative recovery nutrition.

Why TDEE matters globally: A TDEE calculator prevents the two most common errors — chronic under-fueling in endurance athletes causing Relative Energy Deficiency (RED-S), and over-fueling in sedentary rehabilitation patients. By using an accurate macro calculator for weight loss, you create a deficit of 300-500 kcal, preserving metabolic rate and lean mass when protein is 1.8-2.4 g per kg body weight. For maintenance, matching TDEE stabilizes energy availability. For muscle gain, a modest surplus of +250-400 kcal with high carbohydrate supports progressive overload without excessive fat gain.

Macro split science: Protein provides 4 kcal/g and drives muscle protein synthesis; athletes need 1.6-2.2 g/kg, general population 0.8-1.2 g/kg but higher during weight loss. Carbohydrates (4 kcal/g) are primary fuel for high-intensity field sports and brain function; endurance athletes thrive on 4-8 g/kg, team sports 3-6 g/kg. Fats (9 kcal/g) support hormones and fat-soluble vitamins; minimum 0.6-1.0 g/kg, typically 20-35% of calories. This Mifflin-St Jeor calculator auto-balances these splits and instantly converts percentages to grams, making daily calorie needs actionable.

GLOBAL FAQ • TDEE
How accurate is Mifflin-St Jeor vs other formulas?

Most accurate for 82% of population within 10% of lab-measured BMR, outperforming Harris-Benedict. Recommended by ACSM, Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics.

Best macro split for fat loss?

High protein 40%, carbs 30%, fats 30% at calorie deficit preserves muscle, improves satiety. Adjust carbs up if endurance training.

How much deficit for safe weight loss?

300-500 kcal below TDEE = 0.25-0.5kg/week. Don't drop below 1200/1500 kcal without medical supervision. Keep protein high + resistance training.
