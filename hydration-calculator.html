ENDURANCE HYDRATION SCIENCE • ACSM 2024

Preventing Dehydration During High-Intensity Field Events

Dehydration of just 2% body mass impairs sprint repeatability, decision-making, and thermoregulation — major precursors to cramp and exertional heat illness in soccer, rugby, Gaelic football, and track. A sweat loss calculator using the validated sports hydration formula quantifies what scales alone cannot: true fluid loss after accounting for drinking during play.

The Math That Saves Performance

The formula — Sweat Rate = (Pre-workout weight − Post-workout weight + Fluid consumed) ÷ Workout duration (hrs) — is the ACSM gold standard. Weigh in before training in dry kit, track every liter or fl oz consumed, weigh out immediately after towel-drying. The result is your endurance athlete fluid replacement target in liters per hour and fluid ounces per hour. Global studies show sweat rates range from 0.4 L/hr in cool labs to 2.5+ L/hr in humid field events.

From Rate to Replacement

During exercise: Aim to replace 80% of losses hourly — 200-300 ml every 15-20 minutes — to avoid gastrointestinal distress; include 0.5-0.7 g sodium per liter and 30-60 g carbohydrate for sessions over 90 minutes. After exercise: ACSM advises 150% recovery replacement over 4-6 hours (e.g., if you lost 1 L, drink 1.5 L) to cover ongoing urine losses.
