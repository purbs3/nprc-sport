How EDD is Calculated and Why Tracking Trimester Milestones Protects Maternal Health

A pregnancy due date calculator is the anchor of prenatal care. Globally, the standard is Naegele’s Rule, described in 1812: EDD = LMP + 1 year − 3 months + 7 days = LMP + 280 days (40 weeks). It assumes ovulation on day 14 of a regular 28-day cycle, so fertilization occurs roughly at gestational week 2. This yields a gestational age timeline from last menstrual period, not conception. For irregular cycles, we adjust: if your cycle is 35 days, ovulation is later, so we add 7 days to Naegele EDD. Our EDD calculator exact dates does that automatically.

The resulting trimester timeline generator divides pregnancy into evidence-based blocks: Trimester 1 (0w0d–13w6d): from LMP to end of organogenesis, high-risk for miscarriage, critical for folic acid, dating ultrasound, NIPT, and nuchal translucency (11–13w6d). Trimester 2 (14w0d–27w6d): organ maturation, anatomy scan (18–20w), glucose tolerance test (24–28w), quickening (16–20w), and viability threshold starting at 24w0d. Trimester 3 (28w0d–40w0d): rapid fetal weight gain, brain myelination, surfactant production, Group B Streptococcus screening (36w), birth planning, and kick counts from 28w.

Why tracking maternal health milestones matters: adherence to this timeline ensures timely ultrasound dating (most accurate 7–13w by CRL), prevents missed screening, identifies fetal growth restriction or preterm risk, and empowers physiotherapy – prenatal pelvic floor training in trimester 2, posture and edema management in trimester 3. FAQ • EDD & TRIMESTERS
How calculate EDD using Naegele?

EDD = LMP +1y -3m +7d = LMP+280d. Example LMP Jan1 2026 → Oct8 2026. Adjust + (cycle-28) days for irregular cycles.

Trimester exact dates?

T1: 0–13w6d, T2: 14w–27w6d, T3: 28w–40w. Our tool shows calendar dates.

Is LMP calculator accurate?

Accurate for regular cycles; early ultrasound CRL is gold standard and overrides if >7d difference.
